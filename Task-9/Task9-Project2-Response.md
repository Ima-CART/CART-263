Noureddine Mazzene’s Sonic Runner
https://rocmazz.github.io/cart263/topics/project2/

Noureddine’s Sonic runner is a project that will make me feel basis. I loved Sonic since I was a child. I have played a lot of Sonic the hedgehog games, so this project made me feel right at home. The game was not to complicated; you just needed to jump and avoid the objects for as long as you can. Depending on how long you can avoid the objects you will get a certain score. The game is a 2D gameplay of sonic running and avoiding the object, there is no instructions on how to avoid the objects, so that was something I had to figure out on my own. The obvious keys to jump were spacebar or the up arrow, and I was correct about the spacebar. I also learned through the code that sonic is also able to do his signature spin to attack his enemies. The attack spin was the “s” button, something I would not have expected. The rest of the coded explains that the game was created using the phaser library. Once again, this is not a library I am used to. From what I have learned, phaser is another library in JavaScript that is used to design 2d html5 games. How everything is used or put together is another thing. But looking at their code, I found it interesting how each JavaScript files were objects. The title, the endgame and the play scene all had constructor. I would have figured they were their own files without the need to be an object. Looking at the play scene, I learned that the tile was one small image and it was attached together to create a long tile. There were also random holes to cause the player to lose. The background was a big image that repeated itself to give the illusion that the game was moving as Sonic was moving. The enemy was also moving and the red obstacle was staying still as Sonic grew near. The movement of everything was the velocity of the canvas. Sonic’s jump and spin were image sequences that made him look like he was moving. They were all tied nicely. The gameplay, although not easy, I would say depended on luck. When Sonic jumps, you had to hope when he lands there would be no holes in the tiles or object waiting for him as he landed. The same can be said about spinning, if you spin a hole or an object may be ahead to fall or crash into. Overall, the game was fun to play and brought back old Sonic memories.
