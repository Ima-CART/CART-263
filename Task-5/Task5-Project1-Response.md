Phillipe's Project 1 
https://philippe-bn.github.io/cart263/projects/project-1/

Phillipe's Fish game or fish project is really interesting. What I found most intriguing about the fish in the water is how they would move like a fish in an aquarium with no specific destination. The fishes swim unbothered until the mouse is moved. At first I did not understand the concept. I figure they moved towards the mouse, once the mouse entered the blue ocean area. However, I later noticed that once the mouse stoped moving the fishes went about their way. The fishes would only follow the mouse once it moves and when it does not mouse it gave me the impression that the mouse no longer exist in the equation. Another noticeable thing was how the fishes, when the mouse was not on the screen would not interact. When they come close to touching each other they would move away in different directions and would not touch eachother. However when the mouse is in the equation, the mouse becomes the focus. The fishes would focus on the mouse even if they touched each other. They did not react to how close they were as long as the mouse was on the screen. From what I believe I understand from the code, the fish.seek(mouse) is the main cause of the fishes focusing on the mouse. As it was added in both the fish and the aquarium js and the seek(mouse) focusing on the position of the mouse cause the fishes to be attracted and drawn in by the mouse. Although I am a bit confused on what made the fishes repelled each other when they were close by, overall I found the overall project to be interesting.

Leah + Norah's Photo Booth
https://norahwilcox.github.io/CART263/projects/project-1/Project1/

Leah and Norah's Project reminds me of a mini online photo booth. Honestly, I didn't even read the titles as it says exactly what it was, a photobooth. Having the old Window XP background was a nice touch, as I missed the background. Focusing on the project itself I loved the different filters that you could add to the photo. The Invert b&W was creepy and most interesting, the others had different shades and hues which gave off different feeling depending on what you mood you were in. I like there was a timer section and you had the options to choose between 3 or 5 seconds like a real camera. To me the best attribute was that you can save the picture you take on your computer. You had the options to download 1 image, all the images and delete one or more images. Once done you can restart the whole camera process. I can't say I fully understand the complete javascript that was used to save, delete or download the pictures. The whole process is interesting and it is amazing how it all comes together and works. But I can't say I understand the whole process to comment more on it.

Ben's Worm Fishing Survival
https://benmsky.github.io/CART263-main/CART263-main/project1/

Ben's Worm Fishing Survival is a real cool game. Using the mouse movement and the mouseclick in order to shoot at your enemy make the game extremely engaging and exciting. I love the option to be able to chose the color of your warm and the colors of your enemies. It reminds me of a character selection screen, but with much less options. When playing the game, there are different waves and the levels get more difficult. But there are small, I think they are bombs or grenades to help destroy your enemies faster and keep the worm alive. The game itself is hard to coordinate on a laptop as you need to move the mouse and shoot at the same time which makes it a bit difficult. Ben added a nice little easter egg in which when you type the word "worm" in the game the worm would be removed from the hook and would be able to move with the arrow keys. The code behind how this takes place is interesting. There seems to be so many sequences to be active when this specific function is trigger. When the word worm is type it activates the wormJump, which I believes release the worm from the hook and then the worm end up being on the bottom. When the worm is at the bottom wormAtBottom activates and the movement changes to the key doen arrow left, right, up and down, for the direction and the speed of the creature. shootprojectile  is the same, but there is a flexibility added to destroying your enemies. This time they coordination of the movement of the worm and the shootprojectile is tricky as you need to focus on the direction of the mouse and the arrow keys for the worm. There is only one life so if you get hit once that is game over. To conclude it all, you are trying to destroy the fish enemies, while also dodging obstacke in order to stay alive. Worm Fishing Survival is an awesome game to play.
